Why FastPen?

🔒100% on-device

Speech recognition runs on your computer. No cloud, no account, no subscription — your voice never leaves your machine.

🌍Two models, 25 languages

Whisper turbo: the most accurate, great for mixed-language dictation too. Parakeet: the fastest, 25 languages with automatic detection. Both download in one click, right from the app.

🗣️Voice commands

Say “enter” — and Enter is pressed. “New line”, “tab” and “backspace” work too: dictate and send entirely hands-free.

✨AI cleanup

An optional local AI (Ollama) polishes the raw transcript and spells your custom words — brand names, jargon — exactly right. This runs on your machine too.

⚡Near real-time

Text appears the moment you pause. Six seconds of speech is transcribed in about 0.2 seconds on a modern CPU.

⌨️Everywhere, no limits

FastPen types straight into whatever app has focus. It's your CPU — no word counts, no monthly quotas, no premium tier.

Tips for best results

🎤Mic at 5–10 cm (2–4 in)

Microphone distance matters more than any setting. A boom headset is the best choice: the distance stays fixed, so the quality stays consistent. We test FastPen with a Poly Blackwire 3210 USB headset.

🧠Pick the model for the job

Whisper turbo delivers the most accurate text, with the recognition language pinned — and handles mixed-language dictation too. Parakeet is the fastest: ideal for slower machines and voice commands.

⏱️Command rhythm

Say voice commands on their own: sentence — short pause — “enter”. The command arrives as its own segment and is recognized reliably.

🎚️Headset? Sensitivity 0.40

With a headset mic, set the VAD threshold to 0.40 in Settings; with a desktop microphone the default 0.50 works well.

Private by design

FastPen contains no analytics, no telemetry and makes no network requests other than downloading the speech model from GitHub on first start. What you say is processed in memory, typed into your app, and forgotten.

And private doesn't mean compromised: measured on identical reference audio, FastPen's local recognition ties the built-in cloud dictation of Windows on meaningful errors.

FAQ

Is it really free?

Yes. FastPen is free to download and use, with no feature gates. If it becomes popular, a paid version with extra features may follow — dictation will stay free.

Does my voice get uploaded anywhere?

No. Recognition runs entirely on your CPU using the open Parakeet v3 model. FastPen works offline once the model is downloaded.

Which languages are supported?

The Parakeet model covers 25 European languages including English, German, French, Spanish, Italian, Polish, Hungarian, Czech, Romanian and Ukrainian, with automatic detection. The Whisper turbo model (downloadable from the app) adds even more languages, and the language can be pinned.

Which model should I pick?

Whisper turbo delivers the most accurate text — we recommend it for prose and for mixed-language dictation alike, with the recognition language pinned. Parakeet (the default) is many times faster and recognizes voice commands most reliably — pick it on slower machines. Both models are downloaded by the app when you select them (~1 GB and ~465 MB).

What are the voice commands?

Said on their own, with a short pause before and after: “enter” → Enter; “new line” → Shift+Enter; “tab” → Tab; “backspace” → deletes the last word. They work most reliably with the Parakeet model.

Can I dictate to AI agents (Claude, Codex)?

That's one of the main use cases: press the hotkey (push-to-talk works great), speak your prompt, then say “enter” — and the message is sent hands-free. For mixed-language prompts use Whisper turbo. Agents understand the raw transcript just fine, so you can leave AI cleanup off.

What are the system requirements?

Windows 10 (version 1803 or newer) or Windows 11, 64-bit. Any reasonably modern CPU works — no GPU needed. About 700 MB of disk space with the Parakeet model (about 1.7 GB with Whisper turbo as well). A separate build for older 32-bit Windows is also available — use the Parakeet model there.

Can I use Bluetooth earbuds?

Not recommended for dictation. When the earbuds' microphone is active, Bluetooth switches to the hands-free profile with heavily compressed, phone-call-quality audio — recognition accuracy drops noticeably. On Windows 10 there is no way around this (the higher-quality LE Audio is only available on Windows 11 with compatible earbuds). Use a wired or USB boom headset instead. If you do use Bluetooth: untick “Enable audio enhancements” in the microphone's advanced settings.

Is FastPen open source?

Not currently — but you don't have to take our privacy claims on faith. FastPen makes no network requests except downloading the model on first run, and that's verifiable: watch it with a firewall, or simply block it from the internet entirely. It keeps working offline.

Why does Windows warn me when I run it?

FastPen is a new app from a small developer and isn't code-signed yet, so SmartScreen may show a warning. Click “More info” → “Run anyway”. Code signing is planned.
